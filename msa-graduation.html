/ OVERVIEW

A studio, four theses.

Over their final year, four MSA University students each set their own brief and site, then argued a complete architectural proposal in response. My role was to guide that arc — from first concept and site analysis through the resolution of structure, section and environment, to the boards they defended at the end of the semester.

The four schemes are deliberately different in temperament: an expressive civic roof, a pixel-driven massing, a biophilic treatment environment, and a honeycomb tower. Each is presented below in the student’s own boards.

ABasma  بصمة

Student project — Ahmed Abogresha

A large public scheme gathered beneath a single sweeping timber roof. The undulating canopy sets the character of the project from the approach right through to the main hall, where an expressive parametric ceiling defines the interior.

BPixelating Futures

Student project — Rana Zaki

A development whose massing is built from a pixel logic — a stepped, modular vocabulary carried consistently from the masterplan down to the interior. The result is a stacked, terraced set of volumes that reads as a single system at every scale.

CBiophilic Treatment

Student project — Mostafa Bahrawy

A treatment environment built around planting. Greenery is threaded through the section — terraces, atriums and full-height planted voids — so that the landscape becomes part of the healing programme rather than a decorative layer on top of it.

DOne-Hive

Student project — Ziad Ahmed

A tower drawn from the honeycomb. A hexagonal geometry governs both the structural skin and the way the interior stacks — a single hive logic that gives the scheme its identity.
