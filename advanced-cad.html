/ OVERVIEW

From script to simulation to print.

A group of talented MSA University students produced advanced digital models of complex building designs using Python-based visual scripting. Across the elective summer course they learned to use Rhino and Grasshopper — together with plugins including Kangaroo and LunchBox — to model existing buildings and create digital renders.

They then built solar-energy and thermal-comfort simulations with Ladybug and Honeybee, developing strategies for their chosen case studies toward LEED certification. Finally, the models were 3D-printed as physical, scaled pieces — each project carried from parametric definition through analysis to a fabricated object.
