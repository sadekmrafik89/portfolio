/ THE MODULE

A theory course, argued on the ground.

The module gives students a comprehensive overview of urban theory: the socio-economic aspects of urban design, alternative approaches to urban concepts, and the urban problems particular to developing countries. Alongside the reading, it examines the current issues that affect how urban plans are formulated and implemented.

In parallel, students learn the craft — analysing urban design, classifying urban fabrics, gathering and surveying data, and working through the design procedures and methodologies of real urban settings. Each semester below sets that apparatus against a single neglected Cairo site. The proposals shown are the students’ own work.

ASpring 2024 · The Tulunid Waterwheel

Al-Sayeda Zeinab, Cairo

The Tulunid waterwheel at Al-Sayeda Zeinab is a significant heritage site that had, over the years, become a dumping ground. The brief asked students to revitalise it as a vibrant urban park for the surrounding residents — a recreational space threading together seating, walking paths and green areas with cultural elements that celebrate the history of the waterwheel. The semester ran from a field trip and resident surveys through analysis and modelling to a resolved landscape proposal.

BSpring 2023 · Souq Bab-el-Louq

Downtown Cairo

Souq Bab-el-Louq — once a bustling market in Downtown Cairo — had fallen into disrepair through years of neglect and under-investment. The brief was to regenerate it: to restore the market to its former standing, preserving its architectural heritage while modernising it for visitors and shoppers. By making a welcoming open space for locals and tourists alike, the scheme aims to lift economic activity in the area and hold on to a valued piece of Cairo’s cultural heritage.

CSpring 2022 · elHosary Square

6 October City

Most of the open space around elHosary is given over to parking, pushing the majority of users out of the public realm. The brief asked students to revitalise elHosary Square as a more pedestrian-friendly centre for 6 October City. In the second stage, students went on to design a residential neighbourhood 200 metres from the square — carrying the public-space strategy through to the grain of everyday housing.
