ECOFORDABUILD

/ OVERVIEW

Affordable housing that spends less energy, water and carbon.

The construction sector accounts for more than a third of global energy demand and roughly 37% of carbon-dioxide emissions. In line with Egypt’s national green-housing initiative — rolling out across six new cities including New October — ECOFORDABUILD reworks the country’s social-housing model around materials and systems that are environmentally sound yet easy and economical to build.

The structure is a simple skeletal frame with walls of interlocking compressed-soil brick. Floors are thin self-levelling concrete sealed with a water-based varnish; roofs use small-span concrete shell domes carried on a network of beams. Interior doors, shutters and awnings are made from treated palm leaves.

Landscaping follows the same logic — timber and DPLM canopies, limestone pathways, and desert planting that needs little to no irrigation.

/ IFC EDGE

Verified against the IFC EDGE standard.

The proposal was assessed with IFC’s EDGE tool, clearing the EDGE Advanced energy threshold and the material standard. Highlights include roughly 2,169 MWh/year of energy saving, 1,719 m³/year of water saving, and 484 tCO²e of embodied-carbon savings in materials.
